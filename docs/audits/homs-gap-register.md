# HOMS Gap Register

## Purpose

This register centralizes the most important current platform gaps discovered during the documentation audit. It is intended to turn audit findings into an execution-ready backlog.

## Status Context

This file focuses on gaps in currently shipped HOMS behavior. It does not restate planned future modules unless the absence of a capability creates confusion in the current product understanding.

| Gap | Domain | Severity | Current Behavior | Target Behavior | Recommended Fix |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Applicant to offer to employee lifecycle inconsistency | Hiring / Employee Management | High | Applicant records, offers, employee conversion, and onboarding are functional but still span multiple paths and legacy assumptions. | One deterministic lifecycle where applicant, offer, employee, and onboarding history stay linked end-to-end. | Consolidate lifecycle orchestration behind one conversion service and one status model; reduce path-specific behavior in UI and connector sync flows. |
| WordPress or LearnDash-first user sync edge cases | Employee Management / Connectors | High | Users can originate in WordPress before clean applicant-to-employee conversion context exists. Recent fixes reduce duplicates, but this path is still inherently sensitive to data timing and source precedence. | A stable reconciliation model where platform-created and connector-created identities converge predictably into one employee record. | Formalize identity precedence rules and document or enforce a single reconciliation service around normalized email plus applicant linkage. |
| Onboarding vs active status depends on sync timing | Employee Management / Training | High | Employee status now respects onboarding-course completion better, but status changes still depend on whether training data and group assignments have synced yet. | Status should reflect a clear business rule, not the accidental order of connector jobs. | Introduce an explicit lifecycle/status resolver that computes from required onboarding obligations and is invoked consistently after relevant writes. |
| Group-assigned courses vs synced progress visibility | Training And Compliance | Medium | Training screens can now backfill assigned courses when progress rows are missing, but the experience still depends on clean `learndash_group_courses` mapping and employee group state. | Assigned training should be visible and explainable even before progress sync, without relying on brittle fallback behavior. | Strengthen group-course mapping governance and centralize merged assigned-plus-progress logic in one reusable training read model. |
| Recurring compliance visibility depends on active group and rule alignment | Recurring Compliance | High | Recurring compliance core flows exist, but empty or partial views still happen when group enrollment, primary group, or rule setup are not aligned. | Compliance obligations should be predictable, explainable, and easier to diagnose from the product surface. | Add stronger diagnostics, configuration validation, and clearer operator guidance around missing group, rule, or anchor conditions. |
| Current logical domains are not yet physical modules | Architecture | Medium | The app has recognizable domains, but the codebase is still one application with mixed boundaries. | Clear module boundaries that reduce cross-domain coupling and make expansion safer. | Refactor toward explicit domain packages or module folders only after the current logical map and lifecycle rules are stabilized. |
| Audit data exists without a full audit-review product surface | Core Platform / Audit | Medium | Backend audit logging is broad, but operators do not yet have a dedicated audit review experience. | Admin users should be able to inspect important operational history without direct database access. | Add an audit review UI or reporting surface scoped to high-value operational tables first. |
| AI capabilities are present but operationally shallow | Hiring / Admin | Low | AI ranking, summarization, onboarding logic, and offer drafting exist as assistive functions, but not as a cohesive operational subsystem. | AI features should have clearer product boundaries, observability, and trust controls. | Define an explicit AI operations surface, narrow supported workflows, and add stronger traceability and review patterns. |

## Expansion Readiness Gaps

These are not current shipped-product defects. They are architecture and product gaps that must be resolved before the planned Staff App, AI-Powered EVV, and Care Operations modules can be safely implemented.

| Gap | Domain | Severity | Current Behavior | Target Behavior | Recommended Fix |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Regulated care-data architecture not approved | Care Operations / Compliance Architecture | High | Current documentation blocks regulated care-data collection pending separate approval. | Approved architecture for EVV notes, visit notes, participant or client data, incident reports, and any PHI or ePHI-sensitive data. | Define HIPAA or PHIPA posture, BAA and vendor requirements, hosting region policy, RLS, audit model, retention, and access controls before native regulated-care workflows launch. |
| Staff App boundary not yet implemented | Care Operations / Staff App | High | HOMS has no current caregiver-facing Staff App module. | Staff App captures readiness, clock-in or clock-out, GPS, notes, issues, and offline sync while HOMS remains system of record. | Create Staff App PRD, data model, Edge Function boundary, RLS policies, and design system before implementation. |
| EVV governance broker not yet defined | EVV Governance & Integrations | High | EVV is planned, but no broker or adapters exist. | HOMS normalizes official EVV records and submits through backend adapters to Sandata, HHAeXchange, and state systems. | Define normalized EVV schema, broker contract, adapter interface, submission statuses, rejection handling, and reconciliation model. |
| Open shift recovery workflow not yet modeled | Care Operations / Scheduling | Medium | HOMS has no current cancellation-to-replacement workflow. | When staff cancel or become unavailable, HOMS finds eligible staff, broadcasts open shift, receives accept or decline, and routes reassignment to admin approval. | Define open shift request and candidate tables, eligibility filters, notification rules, and admin approval workflow. |
| AI governance for field operations not yet defined | Intelligence / Agentic Operations | Medium | AI exists as assistive hiring and admin slices, but not as a field-operations agentic subsystem. | Rules engine handles compliance-critical actions; AI assists with summaries, messages, note improvement, and risk recommendations; humans approve high-impact actions. | Define AI boundaries, audit logging, model and tool call tracing, note-approval workflow, and prohibited AI actions. |
